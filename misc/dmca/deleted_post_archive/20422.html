Yes, there are other sources.

The manga has a different, more revealing ending compared to the anime which just concluded. The ending in the manga also fuses more completely with the sequel, Tokyo Ghoul:re, which began serialization late 2014. The best thing to do would be to go back and read the last portion of the manga to get a different, more conclusive ending.

Pertaining to Tokyo Ghoul:re

Basically, as far as the manga is concerned, Kaneki:

... is now an amnesiac of sorts and is now known as Haise Sasaki, a Rank 1 investigator and the leader of a special unit of investigators at the Commission of Counter Ghoul(CCG). All the members of that unit underwent similar surgeries as him, allowing them to obtain the special abilities of ghouls in order to fight them, but still being able to live as normal humans.

It seems that a lot of people want to simply write this off as speculation, with no proof that Haise Sasaki is indeed Ken Kaneki's new identity in the sequel who survived the conclusive battle with Arima and is now serving as a mentor at CCG under Akira Mado 2 years later.

Below are some brief hints from chapter 8 volume 1 (which has Sasaki on the cover) of Tokyo Ghoul:re without revealing too much. I find these to be a bit more than mere 'speculation'.

Haise Sasaki begins to notice past self:



Note the mention of a 2 year time span and an amnesia like state questioning about a past life of friends and family, as well as paternalistic Arima:

In summary, to answer your question,

... they did fight, and Kaneki did lose. While he did not die, the aftermath of the fight resulted in him having an amnesia and developing a new identity in a new exciting plot for the sequel, Tokyo Ghoul:re.

It's impossible to tell what they would do in the animated adaptation until its release, although I'm sure it will follow along the same line of plot.

Warning: The following is not only a spoiler from the manga, but also considerably graphic and perhaps not suitable for everyone.

In the Tokyo Ghoul manga, which tells the events somewhat differently and places less focus on Kaneki, Kaneki and Arima had a brutal fight. Kaneki ended up losing, and Arima used brutal methods including eye gouging to defeat him. It is implied at this point that Kaneki is dead.

It remains speculation to say whether or not Kaneki actually survived and continued his story in Tokyo Ghoul:re.
